ğŸ“Š Inter-City Service Demand Forecasting Challenge

Background

A large-scale Inter-City Service Management Platform operates scheduled services between multiple operational hubs across different regions. These services represent standardized inter-hub operations such as managed transfers, capacity allocations, or pre-planned service windows.

Prior to each scheduled service date, users interact with the platform by:

  • Exploring available services
  • Generating interest signals
  • Making advance commitments over time

These interactions provide early indicators of eventual service utilization. However, demand patterns are influenced by multiple factors such as calendar effects, seasonal variations, regional characteristics, and lead-time behavior. Importantly, not all temporal or regional factors impact demand uniformly, making accurate forecasting a complex and non-trivial problem.

ğŸ¯ Problem Objective

The objective of this challenge is to develop a machine learning model that accurately forecasts final service demand for scheduled inter-city services.

Forecasting Requirement

For each service defined by an origin hub, destination hub, and scheduled service date, participants must predict the final number of service units consumed, 15 days before the scheduled service date.

Example

If a service between Hub A â†’ Hub B is scheduled on 30-Jan-2025, the model must predict the final service utilization using only the data available up to 15-Jan-2025.

Participants are provided with historical data from the platform, consisting of both aggregated service records and interaction-level logs.

The datasets include:

  • Scheduled service dates
  • Origin and destination hub identifiers
  • Regional and tier-level attributes
  • Time-dependent cumulative demand signals
  • Final realized service consumption

ğŸ§  Key Challenges

  • Demand accumulation patterns as the service date approaches
  • Regional and hub-level behavioral differences
  • Lead-time effects on forecasting accuracy
